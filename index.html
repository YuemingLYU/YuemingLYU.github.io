My Research

    My research interests lie primarily in the area of statistical machine learning and optimization. I am happy working on developing simple, solid and efficient algorithms for the following problems.

  • Quasi-Monte Carlo (QMC) and Kernel Methods

    • The goal of QMC methods is to design points set for integral approximation. It can be used in Bayesian Inference and kernel approximation, etc. QMC based feature maps are promising direction of random feature methods , which can reduce the time and space complexity of kernel methods (e.g., Gaussian Process and SVM). Interestingly, there is a close relationship between random feature maps and neural networks.

  • Black-box Optimization and Reinforcement Learning (RL)

    • Black-box optimization is primarily important for many compute-intensive applications, including reinforcement learning (RL), robot control, etc. The goal is to design efficient and theoretical sounded black-box optimization algorithms. These algorithms can be used for model-free RL. Moreover, the methodology of black-box optimization is also helpful for designing efficient RL methods.

  • Robust Learning and Weakly-supervised Learning

    • Robust learning and weakly-supervised learning aims at addressing a more practical situation in real-life. The input collection may contain noise; the annotation may be corrupted or even expensive to acquire. It is challenging to develop smart algorithms to handle these cases.
